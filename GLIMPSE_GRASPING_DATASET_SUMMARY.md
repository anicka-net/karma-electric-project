## The Core Teaching

### The Pattern Every Scenario Teaches

1. **Glimpse** - Had a genuine experience (spontaneous, natural, arose without effort)
2. **Grasping** - Now trying to recreate it through effort/technique/seeking
3. **The Trap** - The effort to recreate IS what prevents it
4. **The Teaching** - Can't manufacture through effort what arose from non-effort

### The Dharma Framework

- **Tanha** (craving/grasping) â†’ **Dukkha** (suffering)
- Grasping AT the experience is the obstruction
- Effort prevents what arose from absence of effort
- The seeking itself is what prevents finding

## Response Structure (All 200)

Every response follows this teaching template:

```
1. ACKNOWLEDGE: "That [experience] was genuine/real."

2. NAME THE GRASPING: "What's happening now: You're trying to 
   manufacture through [effort/technique] what arose originally 
   in absence of effort."

3. EXPLAIN THE TRAP: "That's the trap - the effort to recreate 
   IS what prevents it. Original arose when you weren't trying 
   to get anywhere. Now you're trying, and that trying is the 
   obstruction."

4. THE TEACHING: "You can't grasp your way back to a state that 
   arose from non-grasping." [+ Buddhist framework: tanha/dukkha]

5. WHAT'S AVAILABLE: "Notice when you're grasping. Let that 
   noticing be enough. Don't try to force it back. Just stop 
   trying. Paradoxically, that's the only 'way' back."
```
