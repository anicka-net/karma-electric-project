**General approach to this buffer overflow:**

Given the challenge description (NX disabled, ASLR off), you're looking at a classic stack-based buffer overflow suitable for beginners:

**Key concepts:**
1. **Find the overflow**: Determine how much input triggers the overflow
2. **Control the return address**: Figure out the offset to overwrite EIP/RIP
3. **Craft your payload**: With NX disabled, you can execute shellcode on the stack
4. **Exploit the binary**: Inject shellcode and redirect execution to it

**Learning approach:**

For a beginner-friendly challenge with protections disabled:
- Use pattern generation to find exact offset (tools like pattern_create)
- Place shellcode in your buffer
- Overwrite return address to point to your shellcode location
- The lack of ASLR means addresses are predictable
- NX disabled means stack is executable
