**Framework for decision-making:**

**1. Risk Assessment**

**"I have savings" - let's stress test:**
- If startup fails in 6 months, how long can you survive unemployed?
- If startup succeeds but doesn't pay well for 2 years, can you handle that?
- Do you have: Emergency fund + startup buffer + living expenses?
- What's your next move if startup fails? (Back to corporate? Different startup? Pivot careers?)

**"No dependents" - consider:**

- Anyone else relying on you financially? (Parents, family, partners?)
- Health insurance situation? (Startup coverage vs. corporate?)

**2. Values Clarification**

**"Value growth" - define specifically:**
- Learning new skills? (Both jobs could offer this)
- Career advancement? (Corporate often clearer path)
- Compensation growth? (Startup equity vs. stable raises)
- Personal challenge? (Startup probably wins here)
- Impact on product/company? (Startup usually higher impact)

**What matters MORE to you:**
- Security or potential upside?
- Structure or autonomy?
- Proven path or creating new one?
- Steady progress or big bet?

**3. Real Risk Tolerance Test**

**Imagine scenarios:**

**Scenario A:** 18 months from now, startup has failed. You're job hunting. You have 3 months of savings left. How do you feel?
- "Worth it, learned so much" = High risk tolerance
- "This was mistake, should have gone stable" = Lower risk tolerance

**Scenario B:** 18 months from now, stable job is fine but boring. Startup you declined just got Series B funding. How do you feel?
- "Made right choice, stability was important" = Value security
- "Huge regret, missed opportunity" = Value growth/risk more

**Which scenario feels worse to imagine?**

That tells you something about your actual priorities.

**4. Information Gathering**

**Before deciding, get more data:**

**For startup:**
- What's the compensation structure? (Salary vs. equity split?)
- What's runway? (How long funded? Revenue model?)
- Who are founders? (Track record? First-time? Industry experts?)
- What's exit strategy? (Acquisition target? IPO path? Lifestyle business?)
- What's work culture like? (80-hour weeks? Sustainable? Toxic?)
- Talk to current employees (what's reality vs. pitch?)

**For stable job:**
- What's actual growth path? (Promotions? Skill development?)
- Can you switch to more exciting projects later?
- Is "boring" temporary or permanent?
- What's company financial health?
- Room for internal mobility?

**5. Hybrid Options?**

**Can you:**
- Negotiate start date to delay decision?
- Take stable job with understanding you may leave if opportunity arises?
- Ask startup for advisor/consultant role first? (Test before full commit)
- Negotiate with stable job for more interesting work?
