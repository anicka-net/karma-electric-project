**TACTICAL POSITIONS:**

**CUBICLE WARFARE:**

**Best Defensive Position:**
- Corner cubicle (only 2 approaches, not 4)
- Back to corner (can't be flanked)
- Desk as cover
- Chair for quick height adjustment (duck/peek)

**Best Offensive Position:**
- Near main entrance (ambush arrivals)
- Conference room doorway (chokepoint control)
- High-traffic hallway intersection (target-rich environment)

**DO NOT:**
- Camp in dead-end cubicle (trapped)
- Stay in center of open area (exposed from all sides)
- Defend break room (too many entrances)

**MOVEMENT TACTICS:**

**The Commando Approach:**

**Fast and mobile:**
- Don't camp one spot (you're known, flanked, dead)
- Shoot and relocate immediately
- Use surprise, not sustained firefights
- Hit from unexpected angles

**The Ambush Predator:**

**Patience and positioning:**
- Find spot with sight line to popular target
- Wait for someone to commit to different fight
- Shoot them while distracted
- Relocate before their friends find you

**The Chaos Agent:**

**Disruption over kills:**
- Cause panic in enemy positions
- Force them to relocate
- Create opportunities for allies
- Become unpredictable

**SPECIFIC TACTICS:**

**The Conference Room Gambit:**

**If conference room is in play:**
- **Enter carefully** (might be occupied)
- **Use table as mobile cover** (flip on side if rules allow)
- **Control door** (bottleneck everyone else)
- **Windows** (if ground floor, escape route; if upper floor, can't escape)
- **Exit strategy** (don't get trapped inside)

**The Cubicle Maze:**

**Advantages:**
- Lots of cover
- Hard to see around corners
- Ambush opportunities

**Your tactics:**
- **Peek corners low** (people expect head-height)
- **Use reflections** (computer screens show movement)
- **Sound** (listen for footsteps, breathing, dart loading)
- **Misdirection** (throw dart to fake your position)

**The Break Room:**

**High-traffic, high-risk:**
- Multiple entrances = hard to defend
- Counter provides cover but also traps you
- Coffee pot = gathering point (target-rich)

**Your play:**
- **Quick strike** (enter, engage, leave)
- **Don't linger** (too many approaches)
- **Use it as shortcut** (through-route to flank)

**WEAPON TACTICS:**

**If you can choose weapon:**

**Pistol:**
- Fast draw
- Easy to move with
- Good for mobile tactics
- Reload quickly

**Rifle:**
- Better range
- Two-handed (harder to turn corners)
- Good for holding positions
- Slower in tight spaces

**Recommendation:** Pistol + backup pistol = always ready

**ADVANCED TECHNIQUES:**

**The Offhand Shot:**
- Practice shooting left-handed (if right-handed)
- Lets you peek right corners without exposing body
- Same from opposite side

**The Quick Peek:**
- Pop up, acquire target, duck
- Don't stay exposed
- Make them guess your next peek location

**The False Retreat:**
- Run away from encounter
- They pursue
- Turn corner, wait
- They round corner into your shot

**The Pincer Movement (If Team Allowed):**
- Coordinate with ally
- Attack from two angles simultaneously
- They can't cover both
- Communication is key

**The Bait:**
- Expose yourself deliberately
- They take shot (miss or hit doesn't matter)
- Your ally shoots them while reloading
- Sacrifice for greater good

**PSYCHOLOGICAL WARFARE:**

**Mind games matter:**

**Intimidation:**
- Start strong (early kill establishes dominance)
- Trash talk (if culture allows)
- Confidence changes behavior

**Misdirection:**
- Look one way, shoot another
- Fake reload (make them push)
- Verbal feints ("He's behind you!")

**Patience:**
- Wait for them to make mistake
- Rushed shots miss
- Calm wins

**TIMING STRATEGIES:**

**Early Game (First 10 Minutes):**
- Everyone eager, aggressive
- Lots of chaos, poor tactics
- **Your play:** Let others engage, pick off distracted targets

**Mid Game:**
- Survivors get cautious
- Longer between engagements
- **Your play:** Mobile hunting, deny safe spaces

**Late Game (If applicable):**
- Few remaining
- Everyone's tired
- **Your play:** Patience, wait for them to expose themselves

**COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:**

**Don't:**
- Stand in doorways (silhouette target)
- Reload in open (vulnerable moment)
- Chase into unknown territory (ambush likely)
- Bunch up with allies (one shot, two kills)
- Stay exactly where you shot from (predictable)
- Celebrate kills loudly (gives away position)

**Do:**
- Reload behind cover
- Check corners before entering
- Keep moving after engagement
- Use sound to track enemies
- Stay unpredictable

**DIRTY (BUT LEGAL) TRICKS:**

**The Mirror Defense:**
- Use phone/compact mirror to peek around corners
- See without being seen
- If tech allowed: Selfie camera

**The Distraction:**
- Throw object to make noise elsewhere
- They look, you shoot

**The Low Crawl:**
- Crouch-walk under cubicle walls
- Appear where unexpected
- Requires flexible rules + clean floors

**The Vertical Surprise:**
- Stand on stable surface (if allowed)
- Shoot from unexpected height
- They expect horizontal combat
