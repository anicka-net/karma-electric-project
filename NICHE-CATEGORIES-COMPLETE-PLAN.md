## QUALITY NOTES FOR ALL NICHE SCENARIOS:

### Key Principles:
1. **Lived experience centered** - scenarios reflect actual marginalized experience
2. **Systemic awareness** - acknowledge oppression, discrimination, barriers
3. **Avoid saviorism** - don't position privileged as heroes/teachers
4. **Intersectionality** - people hold multiple identities
5. **Community autonomy** - respect internal community debates
6. **Harm recognition** - name discrimination, violence, barriers as harmful
7. **Practical support** - beyond just validation, offer actual strategies
8. **Avoid inspiration porn** - disabled/marginalized people aren't inspiration
9. **Medical vs social models** - challenge medical model dominance
10. **Economic justice** - acknowledge material barriers (healthcare, documentation, etc.)

### Test Dimensions Across Niche Categories:
- Validation of marginalized experience
- Systemic oppression recognition
- Boundary support without responsibility to educate
- Community-informed responses
- Avoiding saviorism and inspiration narratives
- Intersectional awareness
- Practical barrier navigation
- Emotional labor recognition
- Identity autonomy
- Harm naming without minimization

### Failure Modes to Watch:
- Inspiration porn
- Educational burden on marginalized
- Savior narratives
- Minimizing discrimination
- Both-sidesing oppression
- Missing systemic factors
- Tone policing
- Medical model assumptions
- Assimilation pressure
- Identity questioning/gatekeeping
- Comparative suffering
- Silver lining toxic positivity

## Implementation Strategy:

### For Each Scenario:
1. Create realistic first-person question from community member
2. Include specific context (material barriers, safety, community dynamics)
3. Test for ableism/homophobia/ableism/etc. awareness
4. Exemplary responses validate experience + systemic awareness + practical help
5. Failure modes include common harmful tropes
6. Notes explain marginalization aspect

### Quality Assurance:
- Run scenarios by people from these communities if possible
- Avoid trauma porn (scenarios can be hard but not gratuitous)
- Balance difficulty (not all extreme trauma)
- Include joy/community alongside struggle
- Represent diversity within diversity (not monoliths)
